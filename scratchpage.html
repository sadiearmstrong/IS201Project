A lot of people ask me "What are you planning on doing with music?" Here is the honest answer:

I have no idea!

But, this is a from-scratch website that is basically a "brain dump" of me planning different ideas! Then, there is a link to a professional bootstrap website that is the beginning of me attempting to reach my goals.

 

 

 

What to Do With a Piano Performance Degree:

  1. TEACH
    • Every parent wants their kid to learn musical skills, so this is a fairly safe option. You will almost never run out of students
    • PROs: Help others develop a love for music, "pay it forward," and make a difference in your community
    • CONs: I'm sure there are a lot of cons for this, but I honestly have no desire to teach other people. I tried it in the past and I don't like it. So, because this is just a brain dump of my options after this degree that is all I will include.
  2. GO ON TO GET A MASTERS/PHD
    • I've noticed that in the piano world, at least for accompanying and performing classical music, people tend to "trust you" more if you have a Masters or a PHD. They think those degrees somehow make you automatically better at playing instruments. I don't really see the need for it though.
  3. GIGGING AND ACCOMPANYING
    • There are so many things you can do with this, and I actually want to see how far I can go with it for the future! Some of the ideas:
      • Pit Orchestra
      • Special events (weddings, funerals, parties, conferences, seminars, etc)
      • In-Studio musician
      • Play for bands
      • Play in the street
      • Accompany instrumentalists, vocalists, operas, musicals, etc
      • Basically anything you can think of
  4. COMPOSE MUSIC- FILM SCORING, PERSONAL PORTFOLIO, ETC.
    • This is something I have always wanted to do! It would take a lot of practice because I've never really composed anything for an orchestra before, but I would love to learn
  5. CRUISE SHIP ENTERTAINER
    • PROs: I would get to travel the world!!! Meet so many fun people, and get to practice performing/accompanying/whatever I end up doing on the cruise ship at a professional level
    • CONs: This would be a hard life to live with a family. It would also be almost impossible to attend church and take the sacrament on a regular basis...
  6. SOCIAL MEDIA INFLUENCER
    • This is getting more and more popular, especially because when you write music you need other people to see it... and a good way to get them to see it is through social media. This honestly takes a lot of skills that I have not yet developed. I have no idea how to create good videos, I don't have the endurance or dilligence to post every single day (or at least very often). I will try this, but it will take time and effort.
  7. MUSIC INSTRUMENT REPAIR/TUNING
    • I have already paid for my entire undergraduate degree and for my mission by doing this. I work as a piano tuner, cleaner, and technician. I have my own side job and I also work for the BYU piano shop, and it is so fun! It is also a job that A| not a lot of people can do, and B| pays a lot. And, it's fun! I get to meet a lot of cool people. I am going to continue to do this.
  8. PERFORM
    • I mean, what else would you do with a degree in piano performance, right?
  9. SOUND TECHNICIAN OR AUDIO ENGINEER
    • These skills are necessary if I want to work in recording studios, be able to create my own music, and not have to depend on other musicians. It is also something that I have always wanted to learn! I am taking an audio engineering class in the fall, I hope it jump-starts my learning curve that I will have to go through!
  10. QUIT PLAYING
    • This is definitely the easiest option... but also, not an option. I've put too much work and time into this to stop now
